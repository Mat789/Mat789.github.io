Welcome

The Workshop on RECENT ACHIEVEMENT AND PERSPECTIVE DIRECTIONS IN SLIDING MODE CONTROL: SESSION 2: Higher Order Sliding Mode based observers and corresponding output feedback controllers (CDC 2025) brings together researchers and practi.

Scope & Topics

The goal of the track is to reflect the main lines of investigation in Sliding Mode Control including:

  • Homogeneous and bi-homogeneous sliding mode algorithms (continuous and discontinuous)
  • Sliding mode-based observation, identification, fault detection and fault-tolerant control
  • Chattering analysis and adjustment in both frequency domain and state-space
  • Discrete sliding modes
  • Adaptation of sliding mode control algorithms
  • Applications of sliding mode control: control of underactuated systems, aerospace applications, networked control, event-triggered control, and others

Track Organizer

Dr. Leonid Fridman
Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM)

Biography:

Leonid Fridman (Senior Member, IEEE) received the M.S. degree in Mathematics from Kuibyshev (Samara) State University, Samara, Russia, in 1976, the Ph.D. degree in Applied Mathematics from the Institute of Control Science, Moscow, Russia, in 1988, and the D.Sc. degree in Control Science from Moscow State University of Mathematics and Electronics, Moscow, in 1998.

In 2002, he joined the Department of Control Engineering and Robotics, Division of Electrical Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM), Mexico City, Mexico. He has been an Invited Professor in more than 20 universities and research laboratories in Argentina, Australia, Austria, China, France, Germany, Italy, Israel, India, and Spain.

He is a co-author and co-editor of 12 books and 19 special issues devoted to sliding-mode control. His main research interest is sliding mode control.

Dr. Fridman was a recipient of the Harold Chestnut Control Engineering Textbook Prize for the co-authored book Sliding Mode Control and Observation, winner of the National University Prize at UNAM in 2019, and the Scopus Prize for the best cited Mexican scientist in Mathematics and Engineering in 2010.
